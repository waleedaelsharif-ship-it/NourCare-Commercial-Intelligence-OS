# NourCare Pharmacies KSA  
## Commercial Intelligence Operating System (CIOS)

---

## Executive Overview

NourCare Pharmacies is a national-scale pharmacy chain operating across the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

This repository represents a full-year executive simulation of commercial decision architecture across 48 branches and 8 operating regions.

The objective is not dashboard building.  
The objective is capital discipline, decision defensibility, and regional profitability optimization.

---

## Corporate Profile (Simulation Baseline)

- 48 branches
- 2 regional distribution centers
- 12,500 SKUs
- 1.18B SAR annual revenue
- 24.6% gross margin
- 138M SAR total inventory
- 5.8 inventory turnover
- 74-day cash conversion cycle
- 13.8% baseline ROIC

---

## Strategic Context

Despite steady revenue growth, structural weaknesses emerged:

- Dead stock accumulation
- Inventory-sales divergence
- Working capital strain before seasonal peaks
- Supplier concentration risk
- Regional profitability imbalance
- Incentive-driven KPI manipulation exposure

The organization required a disciplined commercial operating system.

---

## System Objective

The Commercial Intelligence Operating System (CIOS) was designed to:

1. Protect Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)
2. Stabilize working capital
3. Quantify trade-offs in every decision
4. Introduce executive defensibility logic
5. Shift culture from revenue obsession to capital efficiency
6. Enforce governance across operational layers

---

## Operating Philosophy

Every decision must answer one question:

Did this increase capital efficiency without compromising supply continuity?

If not, it is rejected.

---

## Structural Architecture

The system is divided into five executive clusters:

### 01 — Operational Control
Capital containment, inventory governance, working capital risk management.

### 02 — Ramadan & Hajj Stress Simulation
Seasonal volatility, peak pressure logistics, overload week decision prioritization.

### 03 — Geographic Intelligence
Regional assortment optimization, logistics-adjusted contribution, ROIC per region.

### 04 — Margin Engineering
SKU rationalization, private label strategy, supplier risk mitigation, promotion ROI discipline.

### 05 — Executive Intelligence Layer
Unified dashboard architecture, AI forecasting pilot, expansion vs capital preservation decisions.

---

## Case Study Framework

Each of the 30 cases in this system follows a strict executive structure:

1. Context (Season + Region + Capital at Risk)
2. Baseline Financial Spine (Mini P&L + Capital Employed)
3. Analytical Diagnosis
4. Political & Behavioral Layer
5. Executive Decision (single sentence)
6. Trade-off Matrix (quantified sacrifice)
7. Counterfactual Scenario
8. Defensibility Test (CFO + CEO challenge)
9. Execution Reality (including failure when applicable)
10. Financial Impact (EBITDA, Cash, ROIC)
11. Governance Ownership
12. Lessons Learned

---

## Trade-off Doctrine

There is no perfect solution.

Every decision must explicitly state:

- What was gained
- What was sacrificed
- What risk was created
- What net capital impact resulted

Illusion-free decision making is mandatory.

---

## Defensibility Principle

Each case must withstand:

### CFO Challenge
Capital logic, financing impact, cost of capital, working capital exposure.

### CEO Challenge
Market position, seasonal readiness, supply continuity, brand risk.

If a decision cannot survive executive interrogation, it is structurally weak.

---

## Seasonal Volatility Layer

The system models real Saudi market dynamics:

- Ramadan demand shifts
- Hajj supply stress
- Back-to-school pediatric spikes
- Summer migration demand redistribution
- Winter respiratory surge
- SAIBOR financing shocks

Operational decisions are evaluated under stress, not stability.

---

## Governance Structure

Decision Owner: Head of Commercial Intelligence  
Financial Oversight: CFO  
Execution Layer: Regional Operations Managers  
Review Cycle: Monthly capital discipline review  

Escalation Trigger:
If inventory growth exceeds sales growth by >5% for 2 consecutive months, executive intervention is activated.
